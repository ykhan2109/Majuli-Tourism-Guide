Why visit Majuli?


→ To visit The Vaishnava Satras founded by Sankardeva.
→ To celebrate the Raas Mahotsav in Majuli
→ To visit during Paal Naam and Ali-Aye-Ligang festival
→ To explore the colourful culture of the tribes
→ To see the Folgu Utsav and Porag Utsav
→ To explore the Mask Making and Pottery Making culture in Majuli


Majuli, an island in the Brahmaputra River in Assam, India, is a unique and captivating destination that offers a range of cultural, natural, and spiritual experiences. Here are some compelling reasons to visit Majuli:
1. Rich Cultural Heritage
Satras (Monasteries): Majuli is the heart of Assamese culture and the center of the Neo-Vaishnavism movement initiated by the saint Srimanta Sankardeva in the 15th century. The island is home to numerous Satras, which are not just monasteries but also centers of art, dance, drama, and music. Traditional Festivals: Festivals like Rasleela, a depiction of the life of Lord Krishna, are celebrated with great enthusiasm and are a sight to behold.
2. Stunning Natural Beauty
Scenic Landscapes: Majuli’s landscape is characterized by lush green rice fields, water meadows, and a tranquil atmosphere. It’s one of the largest river islands in the world and offers a serene and picturesque environment. Bird Watching: The island is a haven for bird watchers, especially during the winter months when migratory birds from various parts of the world visit.
3. Unique Way of Life
Tribal Culture: Majuli is home to several indigenous tribes like the Mising, Deori, and Sonowal Kachari. Visitors can experience their distinct lifestyles, customs, and traditional crafts, such as weaving and pottery. Sustainable Living: The inhabitants of Majuli live in harmony with nature, practicing sustainable agriculture and eco-friendly crafts.
4. Art and Craft
Mask Making: The art of mask making, particularly in the Satra of Samaguri, is a unique cultural aspect of Majuli. These masks are used in traditional dance dramas and are highly intricate and expressive. Pottery and Weaving: The island’s pottery and weaving traditions are noteworthy. The handcrafted items are made using traditional techniques passed down through generations.
5. Spiritual and Tranquil Experience
Peaceful Atmosphere: Majuli offers a serene escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. The peaceful environment is ideal for meditation, reflection, and relaxation. Spiritual Retreats: The Satras offer spiritual retreats and opportunities for introspection and learning about Neo-Vaishnavism and its teachings.
6. Ecotourism
Eco-friendly Tourism: Majuli promotes ecotourism, ensuring that visitors can enjoy its natural beauty without impacting the environment negatively. There are several eco-resorts and homestays that offer sustainable lodging options. Adventure Activities: Activities such as boat rides on the Brahmaputra River, cycling through the countryside, and exploring the island’s various trails can be both adventurous and rejuvenating.
7. Accessibility
Travel Experience: The journey to Majuli itself can be an adventure, involving a ferry ride across the Brahmaputra River, providing stunning views and a sense of adventure. Majuli is a destination that offers a blend of cultural richness, natural beauty, and a peaceful ambiance. It’s a perfect place for those looking to explore traditional lifestyles, immerse themselves in spiritual practices, and enjoy the serene environment of one of the most unique river islands in the world.
